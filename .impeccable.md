## Design Context

### Users
Hotel hosts and stay operators managing the back-office side of room service. They use the admin panel during active operations to monitor live orders, keep the menu accurate, and maintain room access links with minimal friction.

### Brand Personality
Operational, calm, straightforward.

The interface should feel reliable and composed under pressure. It should help staff move quickly without noise, explanation-heavy copy, or decorative clutter.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode only.

Minimal and task-led rather than expressive. Clean surfaces, restrained accent usage, compact information grouping, and strong hierarchy that makes active work obvious first. Avoid loud gradients, teaching panels, and “dashboard theater.”

### Design Principles
1. Put current operational decisions first, especially live orders.
2. Reduce text to labels, signals, and next actions; avoid repeating what the layout already says.
3. Keep navigation and page structure consistent so staff can move by memory.
4. Use contrast, spacing, and grouping for hierarchy before adding more color.
5. Make each screen feel like a focused control surface, not a marketing page.
