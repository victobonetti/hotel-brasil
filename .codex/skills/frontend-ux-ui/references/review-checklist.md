# Frontend UX/UI Review Checklist

Use this checklist when reviewing a screen or proposing a redesign.

## Core Questions

- Is the primary task visible within the first screenful?
- Is the main CTA unmistakable?
- Can a user scan the page in 5 seconds and describe its purpose?
- Does the layout still make sense on a narrow mobile viewport?
- Are loading, empty, and error states intentionally written and styled?

## Hierarchy

- Lead with one headline promise or operational summary.
- Demote secondary metadata with spacing and muted text.
- Avoid equal visual weight across every card.
- Use sections when content mixes metrics, actions, and details.

## Actions

- Keep primary and secondary actions visually distinct.
- Place row-level actions close to the object they affect.
- Avoid hiding common actions behind too many clicks.
- Confirm destructive actions clearly.

## Data-Dense Screens

- Surface totals, counts, and current status first.
- Make sorting, filtering, and pagination understandable without explanation.
- Keep labels concrete.
- Avoid forcing users to compare distant parts of the page.

## Copy

- Prefer short verbs and plain nouns.
- Remove filler words.
- Explain why a user cannot proceed.
- Make success feedback explicit after mutations.

## Polish

- Keep spacing rhythm consistent.
- Use borders, shadows, and color with restraint.
- Ensure tap targets and focus states are obvious.
- Watch for cards or panels that feel decorative rather than informative.
